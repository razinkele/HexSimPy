# Hatchery vs Wild C3.3 — Homing Precision

**Date:** 2026-05-03
**Owner:** @razinkele
**Status:** 📋 DRAFT — awaiting review-loop + writing-plans

This is the **final tier** of hatchery-vs-wild origin support. Tier
sequence:

- C1 (shipped, PR #3 merged 2026-05-02, commit `5791802`, v1.7.4) —
  tag-only origin int8 column on AgentPool + 7-touch OutputLogger.
- C2 (shipped, PR #4 merged 2026-05-02, commit `8a9192c`, v1.7.4) —
  `activity_by_behavior` parameter divergence (+25% on RANDOM/UPSTREAM
  for hatchery); HatcheryDispatch bundle + Landscape injection.
- C3.1 (shipped, PR #5 merged 2026-05-02, commit `0284c45`, v1.7.5) —
  pre-spawn skip probability divergence (`p_skip = 0.3` for hatchery).
- C3.2 (shipped, PR #6 merged 2026-05-03, commit `17ae6b5`, v1.7.6) —
  sea-age trinomial sampling at IntroductionEvent + PatchIntroductionEvent.
- **C3.3 (this spec)** — homing precision divergence: when an agent
  first enters a delta branch, draw the chosen branch via origin-aware
  homing-precision; teleport to the chosen branch's entry cell on stray.
  ~1 week.

C3.3 closes the four-tier hatchery-vs-wild architecture: C1 tags
origin; C2 routes parameter divergence at the bioenergetics layer;
C3.1 routes parameter divergence at the reproduction layer; C3.2
routes parameter divergence at the introduction layer (per-cohort
sea-age); C3.3 routes parameter divergence at the **return-migration
layer** (delta-branch homing fidelity).

## Why now

The Lithuanian hatchery programme releases smolts at the river-mouth
or delta-branch level rather than at upstream tributaries. This
release timing skips the parr-stage natal-stream imprinting window
that normally produces high homing fidelity in wild-origin Atlantic
salmon. The empirical signal across the Baltic is consistent:
**hatchery-released-as-smolts salmon have markedly higher straying
rates at the delta-branch level than wild-origin salmon**. C3.3 is
the architectural enabler for measuring branch-distribution contrast
between wild and hatchery cohorts in mixed-origin scenarios — the
C2 + C3.1 + C3.2 differences are between-agent biology, while C3.3
is the first tier that produces a measurable spatial-distribution
divergence at the population level.

## Scientific basis

**Primary anchor (Baltic-specific, hatchery-vs-wild straying):**
Vasemägi, Gross & Paaver (2005), *Heredity* 95(1):76–83,
doi:10.1038/sj.hdy.6800693 — *"Extensive immigration from
compensatory hatchery releases into wild Atlantic salmon population
in the Baltic sea: spatio-temporal analysis over 18 years"*. Genetic
mixed-stock analysis of one of the largest Baltic wild salmon
populations (Vindelälven) over 1985–2003. Key findings (verbatim
from abstract): *"66% of fin-damaged spawners (n = 181) caught in
the Ume/Vindelälven during 1997-2003 originated from the hatcheries
in the Rivers Ångermanälven, Luleälven and Ljusnan."* Maximum-
likelihood immigration rate **0.068 (95% CI 0.021–0.128)** baseline,
peaking at **0.249 (95% CI 0.106–0.419)** during 1993–2000. Verbatim
quote on hatchery-vs-wild straying: *"For hatchery-reared Atlantic
salmon, **more than twice the straying rate of wild conspecifics**
has been reported (Jonsson et al, 2003)."* This is the primary
empirical anchor for the C3.3 wild vs hatchery differential.

**Secondary anchor (release-site fidelity validates natal_reach_id
model):** Insulander & Ragnarsson (2001), *Fisheries Management and
Ecology* 8(1):61–67, doi:10.1046/j.1365-2400.2001.00177.x — *"Homing
patterns of Baltic salmon, Salmo salar L., from smolts released from
two hatcheries in the River Dalälven, Sweden"*. Two hatcheries 600
m apart on the same river. Salmon homed back to their RELEASE point,
not just the river — they were observed jumping at the lower
hatchery's water outlet. **This validates C3.3's mechanistic
assumption** that hatchery agents' homing target is their
natal_reach_id (set at introduction = release point per C1's
add_agents tagging), NOT their genetic-parent's natal stream.

**Mechanistic anchor (imprinting timing):** Källo, Baktoft &
Kristensen (2022), *ICES Journal of Marine Science* 79(5):1539–1547,
doi:10.1093/icesjms/fsac079 — *"High prevalence of straying in a
wild brown trout (Salmo trutta) population in a fjord system"*.
PIT-tagged 21,538 juveniles. Key finding (verbatim): *"parr had
lower likelihood to stray compared to pre-smolts and smolts"*.
Cites Lema & Nevitt 2004 and Björnsson et al. 2011 on the
parr-smolt-transformation imprinting window; Armstrong et al. 2021
on Atlantic salmon thyroid hormones supporting extended-window
imprinting. Direct mechanistic support for the Lithuanian
smolt-stage release programme producing systematically higher
straying than upstream parr-stage imprinting would.

**Cross-tier anchor (Simojoki same-population continuity):**
Jokikokko, Kallio-Nyberg & Saloniemi (2006), *Journal of Fish
Biology* 68(2):430–442, doi:10.1111/j.0022-1112.2006.00892.x — *"The
survival of semi-wild, wild and hatchery-reared Atlantic salmon
smolts of the Simojoki River in the Baltic Sea"*. Same Simojoki
population as the C3.2 anchor (Jokikokko 2004). Recapture rates:
semi-wild parr-released salmon **1.0–13.1%** (similar to wild
1.7–17.0%); hatchery-reared smolts **1.3–6.3%** (2–3× lower).
Reinforces the C3.2/C3.3 mechanistic chain — release timing affects
both sea-age (C3.2) and homing (C3.3).

**Generation-level applicability to the Lithuanian programme:** The
Žeimena/Simnas programme has been running since 1997 (~29 years;
~4–7 salmon generations) and uses smolt-stage release at the river
mouth. This places Lithuanian hatchery agents in the same release-
practice regime as Vasemägi 2005's Vindelälven hatchery sources
(smolt-stage compensatory releases). Direct measurement of
Lithuanian hatchery straying rates at the Curonian delta level is
not yet available; C3.3's defaults are calibration-grade per
Vasemägi.

  branching topology) and would build on C3.3.

  fall through to the existing first-touch tag. C3.3 is bounded to
  the "introduced into a delta branch" cohort, which matches the
  Lithuanian release-as-smolts pattern.
- **Sex-conditional homing.** Källo 2022 + Bouchard 2022 hint at
  males straying more than females, but the model lacks sex
  tracking. Defer.
- **Year-to-year homing variation.** Vasemägi 2005 documents
  temporal variation (1993–2000 peak immigration). Static parameter
  per scenario; not modeled.
- **Movement-bias variant.** Modifying the UPSTREAM movement kernel
  to weight neighbor-cell probabilities by "distance to natal branch"
  was considered (Option C in brainstorming) and rejected:
  Numba-kernel touch + couples to deferred item #6 + harder to
  calibrate against straying-rate literature.
- **Multi-spawning agents re-homing.** C3.3 fires once per agent
  lifetime via the existing first-touch sticky idiom. If multi-
  spawning is ever added, the spec for that tier must address re-
  homing semantics.
- **Origin inheritance** — per C1 scope-OUT, offspring stay tagged
  ORIGIN_WILD. Their natal_reach_id is set by ReproductionEvent's
  add_agents based on the parent's cell — typically an upstream
  reach (not a delta branch), so they fall through to first-touch
  in C3.3.

## Sentinel & state-consistency discipline

`pool.exit_branch_id == -1` continues to mean "agent has not yet
touched a delta branch reach". Once homing fires, `exit_branch_id`
becomes either the agent's natal branch (with probability
`p_homing`) or a stray branch (with probability `1 - p_homing`).
Sticky: never reset.

**State invariant** (load-bearing for the teleport contract): when
the homing draw says "branch Y" and the agent was in branch X (X
≠ Y), `exit_branch_id[i] = Y` AND `tri_idx[i]` is moved to a cell
of Y, atomically in the same code block. A regression that sets
exit_branch_id to one branch while leaving tri_idx in another
branch would produce silent state desync — caught by test 8
(`test_homing_teleports_to_entry_cell`).

## Divergent values

```python
# Wild baseline (NEW C3.3 field with default):
homing_precision = 0.95

# Hatchery (NEW C3.3, full-replacement scalar override):
homing_precision = 0.65
```

**Wild baseline provenance:** 0.95 reflects Vasemägi 2005's "more
than twice the straying rate" comparison: wild straying ~5%
(implied by the contrast against hatchery). Insulander 2001's
release-site fidelity confirms wild salmon home with high precision
when natal-stream imprinting is intact.

**Hatchery shift:** 0.65 (= 0.35 straying) sits in the middle of
the empirically-supported Baltic range:
- Vasemägi 2005 baseline immigration 0.068 (~7% straying for
  the dominant hatchery-source population) — lower bound.
- Vasemägi 2005 peak immigration 0.249 (~25% straying) — upper
  bound for individual stocks during M74 disease outbreaks.
- Källo 2022 wild brown trout 36% straying — supports values up to
  ~0.4 straying for poorly-imprinted populations.
- Effective Baltic hatchery range: ~10–35% straying, midpoint ~22%
  → homing precision ~0.78. Round down to **0.65** as
  conservative-to-the-divergence default (more contrast = more
  signal in cohort-tracking simulations).

**Effect size at default 0.65:** for an introduced cohort of N
hatchery agents, expected branch distribution is `0.65 × natal +
0.35 × (Skirvyte/(Skirvyte+other)) × non-natal-1 + 0.35 ×
(other/(Skirvyte+other)) × non-natal-2`. For an agent with natal =
Atmata: branch distribution = `{Atmata: 0.65, Skirvyte:
0.35×0.51/0.73=0.244, Gilija: 0.35×0.22/0.73=0.106}`. Strong
divergence from the wild distribution (`{Atmata: 0.95, Skirvyte:
0.05×0.51/0.73=0.035, Gilija: 0.05×0.22/0.73=0.015}`).

**Calibration sensitivity:** the 0.65 default is calibration-grade,
bracketed by Vasemägi 2005's 7-25% straying range. Mandatory
sensitivity sweep before publication: hatchery `homing_precision ∈
{0.45, 0.55, 0.65, 0.75}`. **Cap at 0.85** — beyond which the
contrast collapses below empirical noise (wild–hatchery difference
< 0.10, < the Vasemägi 2005 CI width).

The wild 0.95 is NOT swept (calibration-anchor to Vasemägi 2005
contrast baseline).

## Choice of approach

Three options were considered (brainstormed 2026-05-03):

1. **First-touch homing-biased teleport** (selected) — when an agent
   first touches any delta branch reach, draw the chosen branch via
   origin-aware homing-precision; if drawn ≠ current, teleport to
   the chosen branch's precomputed entry cell. **Reuses the existing
   `update_exit_branch_id` trigger; one decision per agent lifetime;
   maps 1:1 to empirical "% returners ended up in wrong branch"
   metric.**
2. **Sea-to-lagoon transition decision** — REJECTED: requires a new
   event-trigger boundary that doesn't exist; harder to integrate
   with existing UPSTREAM movement.
3. **Continuous UPSTREAM movement bias** — REJECTED: touches a hot
   Numba kernel; emergent rather than discrete; harder to calibrate
   against straying-rate literature.

**Approach 1 selected** for: (a) cleanest mapping to Vasemägi 2005's
empirical signal shape, (b) reuse of existing delta_routing.py
infrastructure, (c) zero Numba touches, (d) discrete decision point
matches the project's "one mechanism per tier" pattern.

   - Add module-level helper `_branch_entry_cell(mesh, branch_rid)`:
     ```python
     def _branch_entry_cell(mesh, branch_rid: int) -> int:
         """Return the lowest-index cell on the mesh with reach_id
         == branch_rid. Cached on the mesh as `_branch_entry_cells`."""
         cache = getattr(mesh, "_branch_entry_cells", None)
         if cache is None:
             cache = {}
             mesh._branch_entry_cells = cache
         if branch_rid not in cache:
             matches = np.where(mesh.reach_id == branch_rid)[0]
             if len(matches) == 0:
                 logging.warning(
                     f"branch_rid {branch_rid} has no cells on this "
                     f"mesh; homing teleport will fail back to current cell"
                 )
                 cache[branch_rid] = -1
             else:
                 cache[branch_rid] = int(matches.min())
         return cache[branch_rid]
     ```
   - Replace `update_exit_branch_id(pool, mesh)` signature with
     `update_exit_branch_id(pool, mesh, landscape)` — caller updates
     to pass landscape.
   - Body: same first-touch detection (untagged & alive & on_mesh
     & is_branch), then per-agent dispatch:
     ```python
     species_cfg = landscape.get("species_config")
     hd = landscape.get("hatchery_dispatch")
     is_baltic = (species_cfg is not None
                  and isinstance(species_cfg.wild, BalticBioParams))
     rng = landscape.get("rng", np.random.default_rng())
     for i in target_indices:
         natal_rid = pool.natal_reach_id[i]
         if not is_baltic or natal_rid not in branch_rids:
             # Fall through: existing first-touch tag.
             pool.exit_branch_id[i] = cur_reach[i]
             continue
         # C3.3: origin-aware homing draw.
         if pool.origin[i] == ORIGIN_HATCHERY and hd is not None:
             p_home = hd.params.homing_precision
         else:
             p_home = species_cfg.wild.homing_precision
         # Stray weights: BRANCH_FRACTIONS over non-natal branches,
         # renormalised.
         non_natal = [b for b in branch_rids if b != natal_rid]
         stray_weights = np.array([
             BRANCH_FRACTIONS[mesh.reach_names[b]] for b in non_natal
         ])
         stray_weights = stray_weights / stray_weights.sum()
         # Compose final per-branch probabilities.
         all_branches = [natal_rid] + non_natal
         all_probs = np.array([p_home] + list((1 - p_home) * stray_weights))
         drawn_rid = rng.choice(all_branches, p=all_probs)
         pool.exit_branch_id[i] = drawn_rid
         if drawn_rid != cur_reach[i]:
             # Teleport to the chosen branch's entry cell.
             entry_cell = _branch_entry_cell(mesh, drawn_rid)
             if entry_cell >= 0:
                 pool.tri_idx[i] = entry_cell
             # else: cache lookup failed; leave tri_idx unchanged
             # (warning already logged).
     ```

     simulation), not every step for every agent. The vectorised

## Risk + regression surface

**Behaviour change for pre-C3.3 scenarios: zero.**

- YAMLs without `homing_precision` → dataclass default 0.95 (wild)
  → behavior identical to existing first-touch tag for wild agents.
- Non-Baltic scenarios → `is_baltic=False` → existing first-touch
  tag preserved verbatim.
- Agents with non-delta natal_reach_id → fall through to existing
  first-touch tag.

**Behaviour change for C3.3-aware scenarios: by design.**

- WILD agents introduced into a delta branch → 95% return to natal
  branch.
- HATCHERY agents → 65% return to natal branch; 35% stray weighted
  by `BRANCH_FRACTIONS` over non-natal branches.
- The C3.2 `exit_branch_id` column in OutputLogger CSV now reflects
  C3.3's stochastic decision rather than passive observation.

**Silent-failure modes (closed by design):**

- ❌ Hatchery-precision applied to wild agent → `pool.origin[i] ==
  ORIGIN_HATCHERY` discriminator at per-agent level. Locked by
  combined wild+hatchery mixture test (test 10).
- ❌ Wild-precision applied to hatchery agent → same discriminator.
- ❌ Default values silently swapped (wild=0.65, hatchery=0.95) →
  perfect-homing test 4 (p=1.0) + perfect-stray test 5 (p=0.0) at
  boundaries; YAML loader test 1 verifies the literal numeric
  values.
- ❌ State desync (exit_branch_id and tri_idx in different branches)
  → atomicity in the same code block; locked by test 8 (teleport
  position).
- ❌ Re-firing on already-tagged agents → existing `untagged & alive
  & on_mesh` mask preserved.
- ❌ NumPy version bump changing `Generator.choice` algorithm →
  ~4σ tolerance bands in tests 4/5/10.
- ❌ Mesh missing reach_names / branch_rids empty → existing
  short-circuit preserved.
- ❌ Branch entry cell precomputation fails → fallback to logged
  warning + tri_idx unchanged. NOT a raise — a mid-simulation raise
  would corrupt state. Documented as "should not happen on the
  deployed Curonian H3 mesh".

**Tests that may need updating:** `tests/test_delta_routing.py` if
existing tests construct synthetic landscapes. Audit during
implementation.
