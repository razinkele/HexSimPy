**Date:** 2026-05-03
**Owner:** @razinkele
**Status:** 📋 DRAFT — awaiting writing-plans

This is the **third tier, second sub-task** of hatchery-vs-wild origin
support. Tier sequence:

- C1 (shipped, PR #3 merged 2026-05-02, commit `5791802`) — tag-only
  origin int8 column on AgentPool + 7-touch OutputLogger.
- C2 (shipped, PR #4 merged 2026-05-02, commit `8a9192c`) —
  `activity_by_behavior` parameter divergence (+25% on RANDOM/UPSTREAM
  for hatchery); HatcheryDispatch bundle + Landscape injection.
- C3.1 (shipped, PR #5 merged 2026-05-02, commit `0284c45`) —
  pre-spawn skip probability divergence (`p_skip = 0.3` for hatchery).
- **C3.2 (this spec)** — sea-age sampling divergence (new `sea_age`

  parameterised by origin-aware `sea_age_distribution`). ~3 days.
- C3.3 (future) — homing precision divergence (modifies migration /
  delta-routing logic).

C3.2 closes the metadata-completeness gap on origin: C1 tags origin,
C2 routes parameter divergence at the bioenergetics layer, C3.1 routes
parameter divergence at the reproduction layer, and C3.2 routes
parameter divergence at the **introduction layer** (the per-cohort
sea-age distribution). Together C1+C2+C3.1+C3.2 form a complete
"hatchery agents are demographically distinguishable from wild agents
in 4 layers" coverage.

## Why now

Sea age (1SW = grilse spending 1 winter at sea, 2SW = "two-sea-winter",
3SW = "three-sea-winter") is the most-cited demographic distinction
between Baltic hatchery-stocked vs wild Atlantic salmon (*Salmo
salar*). Hatchery-stocked smolts are released at predictable size and
condition; wild smolts vary more by year-class and natal stream
quality. The empirical signal across the Baltic is consistent:
**hatchery-origin returners have a higher proportion of 1SW (grilse)
than wild-origin returners**, with a corresponding suppression of
older-sea-age cohorts.

This matters for migration timing, body size at return, fecundity,
and reproductive interaction with the C3.1 pre-spawn skip filter
(younger sea-age fish are smaller and likely interact with C3.1
through the as-yet-unmodeled sex-by-size axis). C3.2 lays the
metadata in place; downstream C3.x tiers can read it.

## Scientific basis

**Primary anchor (Baltic-specific, hatchery-vs-wild contrast):**
Kallio-Nyberg, Jokikokko & Jutila (2004), *Journal of Applied
Ichthyology* 20(2):91–94, doi:10.1111/j.1439-0426.2004.00491.x —
*"Reduction in heritability for sea-age in Atlantic salmon (Salmo
salar L.) of the river Simojoki, Baltic Sea, after a stocking
period"*. Long-term study of the Simojoki population (Bothnian Bay
tributary, the closest unregulated Baltic system to Lithuanian
fisheries) before and after intensive hatchery stocking. The study
documents a **reduction in mean sea age** of returning fish following
the stocking period — i.e. proportionally more 1SW grilse and fewer
2SW/MSW returners after hatchery contributions ramped up. This is
the strongest single empirical anchor for C3.2's intervention shape.

**Secondary anchor (mechanistic / temporal divergence):**
Kallio-Nyberg, Vainikka & Heino (2010), *Journal of Fish Biology*
76(4):807–826, doi:10.1111/j.1095-8649.2009.02520.x —
*"Reproductive ecology of female salmon: temporal isolation between
two ecotypes in the Teno River drainage"*. Documents temporal /
ecotype separation by sea age within Atlantic salmon populations
relevant to interpreting hatchery-vs-wild divergence in mixed-origin
runs.

**Population-scale anchors:** ICES Working Group on Baltic Salmon and
Trout (WGBAST) annual reports (most recent at time of writing:
WGBAST 2024). WGBAST tabulates sea-age composition of Baltic salmon
catches by stock and year and consistently reports higher 1SW
fractions in hatchery-supported stocks than in wild stocks. WGBAST
reports are the population-scale anchor for the wild and hatchery
defaults below; sub-stock granularity is not yet modeled in the IBM.

**Generation-level applicability to the Lithuanian programme:** The
Žeimena/Simnas programme has been running since 1997 (~29 years; ~4-7
salmon generations). This places Lithuanian hatchery fish in a
similar generation-level regime to Kallio-Nyberg 2004's Simojoki
post-stocking decades, making the Simojoki contrast a defensible
analog for Nemunas-basin defaults pending direct Lithuanian
sea-age-by-origin measurement.

**In:**

  default is the trinomial above (35% 1SW, 55% 2SW, 10% 3SW).

  non-empty, integer keys in `{1, 2, 3}`, positive floats, sum within
  `1e-6` of 1.0. Out-of-range raises `ValueError`.
- `_apply_hatchery_overrides` extended: `ALLOWED_OVERRIDE_KEYS` now
  includes `"sea_age_distribution"`. Shallow-merge semantics (mirror
  the existing `activity_by_behavior` pattern). YAML string keys
  coerced to int via the same try/except that protects
  `activity_by_behavior`.
- New `AgentPool.sea_age: int8` field appended to `ARRAY_FIELDS`,
  initialised to `-1` in `__init__` (sentinel = "not yet drawn"; matches
  the `natal_reach_id = -1` and `exit_branch_id = -1` precedent for
  "tag pending"). Default `-1` propagated through `Population.add_agents`

  `PatchIntroductionEvent.execute` (in `salmon_ibm/events_hexsim.py`)

**Out:**

- **Sea-age affecting body size at introduction.** C3.2 only stores
  the metadata; later C3.x tier (or its own future plan) wires
  sea_age into mass/length sampling. Today's `initial_mass_mean`
  remains a single distribution parameter on `IntroductionEvent`.
- **Sea-age affecting fecundity.** Same logic — metadata first,
  consumer wiring later.
- **Sea-age affecting migration timing or homing.** C3.3 (homing
  precision) may consume sea_age but does not need it for its
  initial scope.
- **4SW or older.** Vanishingly rare in modern Baltic returns
  (Kallio-Nyberg 2010 reports MSW >2 fractions trending down in the
  Teno even before hatchery effects); not modeled.
- **Sex-conditional sea-age distributions.** Bouchard 2022 and
  WGBAST both note that sea-age and sex are correlated (males more
  often grilse). The current model doesn't track sex; defer.
- **Year-to-year stochastic variation in sea_age_distribution.**
  WGBAST shows annual variation; C3.2 uses a single fixed
  distribution per scenario.
- **Sub-stock-specific distributions.** The Lithuanian Žeimena and
  Simnas programmes may have different distributions; the IBM
  doesn't yet distinguish sub-stocks within the BalticAtlanticSalmon
  species block.
- **Origin inheritance on reproduction.** Per C1 scope-OUT decision,
  offspring of hatchery parents continue to default `ORIGIN_WILD`
  with `sea_age=-1` (sentinel; offspring are never assigned a
  marine-residency age — they're at-river by definition).
  Reproduction event leaves `sea_age` at -1 for new offspring.
- **C3.3 homing precision.** Separate spec.

## Divergent values

```python
# Wild baseline (NEW C3.2 field with default):
sea_age_distribution = {1: 0.35, 2: 0.55, 3: 0.10}

# Hatchery (NEW C3.2):
sea_age_distribution = {1: 0.55, 2: 0.40, 3: 0.05}
```

**Wild baseline provenance:** 35% 1SW / 55% 2SW / 10% 3SW reflects
WGBAST 2024 ranges for wild-origin Baltic Atlantic salmon stocks,
weighted toward Bothnian Bay / Gulf of Finland populations
(Kallio-Nyberg 2004 Simojoki pre-stocking analog).

**Hatchery shift:** 55% 1SW / 40% 2SW / 5% 3SW represents the
Kallio-Nyberg 2004 Simojoki post-stocking shift: ~+20pp on 1SW with
proportional reduction on 2SW + 3SW. Hatchery 3SW is suppressed more
strongly than 2SW because earlier marine maturation eliminates the
tail of late returners disproportionately.

**Effect size at default:** for an introduced cohort of N hatchery
agents, expected sea_age composition shifts from (35/55/10) → (55/40/5)
relative to the wild scenario. In a mixed scenario with k% hatchery,
the population-level grilse fraction increases by ~`0.20 × k%`. This
compounds with C2's activity divergence (mass-loss differential) and
C3.1's pre-spawn skip (offspring-count differential) to produce a
distinguishable hatchery vs wild signal in cohort tracking.

**Calibration sensitivity:** the hatchery 1SW share of 0.55 is
calibration-grade. Mandatory sensitivity sweep before publication:
**hatchery 1SW at {0.35, 0.45, 0.55, 0.65}** (with 2SW/3SW renormalised
proportionally to keep total = 1.0). **Cap at 0.7** — beyond is
unsupported by Kallio-Nyberg 2004's Simojoki data and would conflate
sea-age effects with maturation-rate effects (a separate biological
signal).

The wild 1SW share of 0.35 is NOT swept (treated as calibration-anchor
to WGBAST 2024 wild baseline; varying it would change what the
contrast is being measured *against*).

## Choice of approach

Three options were considered for the model intervention shape
(brainstormed 2026-05-03):

1. **Metadata-only sea-age tag at introduction, trinomial 1SW/2SW/3SW
   distribution** (selected) — `IntroductionEvent.execute` samples
   `sea_age` from origin-aware distribution; no consumer event yet.
   Mirrors C1's "tag now, consume later" architecture exactly.
2. **Sea-age field PLUS body-size coupling at introduction** —
   sample sea_age, then set `initial_mass_mean` conditional on
   sea_age. **REJECTED for C3.2:** doubles scope; introduces a new
   biological signal (sea_age → size) that is itself
   calibration-sensitive (mass-at-age allometry, region-specific).
   Defer to a future tier with a dedicated spec.
3. **Continuous sea-age (months at sea) with categorical 1SW/2SW/3SW
   binning** — Most flexible. **REJECTED for C3.2:** the empirical
   data (WGBAST, Kallio-Nyberg 2004) is reported as discrete sea-age
   classes; the IBM has no time-of-introduction model that needs
   continuous sea-residence; the int8 storage cost is tiny and
   matches existing `int8` precedent.

**Approach 1 selected** for: (a) cleanest mapping to the empirical
data shape (categorical), (b) symmetry with C1's tag-only metadata
pattern (`origin` int8), (c) zero touch on bioenergetics or migration
hot loops (no Numba kernel impact), (d) the `hd is not None` guard +
default sentinel `-1` keeps existing pre-C3.2 scenarios unaffected,
(e) the same shallow-merge override pattern that C2's
`activity_by_behavior` established generalises naturally to a second
dict-valued override key.

## Risk + regression surface

**Behaviour change for pre-C3.2 scenarios: zero.**

- YAMLs without `sea_age_distribution` → dataclass default
  `{1: 0.35, 2: 0.55, 3: 0.10}` → existing tests still load; the wild
  default is structurally always available.
- The `IntroductionEvent` sea-age sampling block is gated on
  `species_cfg is not None and hasattr(species_cfg.wild,
  "sea_age_distribution")`. Non-Baltic scenarios (legacy `BioParams`
  via `species_cfg.wild` returned by the legacy-path
  `BalticSpeciesConfig`) skip the block entirely (legacy `BioParams`
  has no `sea_age_distribution` attribute).
- `pool.sea_age` initialised to `-1` for offspring + non-Baltic
  scenarios. This is a new column in OutputLogger CSV output; if
  downstream consumers parse the CSV positionally (rather than by
  column name), they'll need a one-time update. Audit before merge.
- `landscape.get("hatchery_dispatch")` returns None for tests without
  C2 setup → `hd is None` branch → falls through to wild distribution
  for WILD agents; HATCHERY agents are blocked earlier by C2's
  IntroductionEvent runtime guard (raised in `IntroductionEvent.execute`
  ABOVE the new C3.2 sampling code).

**Behaviour change for C3.2-aware scenarios: by design.**

- WILD agents drawn from `{1: 0.35, 2: 0.55, 3: 0.10}` at introduction.
- HATCHERY agents drawn from `{1: 0.55, 2: 0.40, 3: 0.05}`.
- `OutputLogger` CSV gains a `sea_age` column; mixed-origin scenarios
  show distinguishable sea-age compositions per origin.

**Silent-failure modes (closed by design):**

- ❌ Legacy non-Baltic BioParams without sea_age_distribution attribute
  silently emits `-1` for all agents → caught by `hasattr` guard:
  attribute missing → C3.2 sampling skipped → all agents stay at the
  `-1` sentinel, which is the documented "not drawn" state. Test
  coverage: no specific test, but pytest's existing non-Baltic
  scenarios (e.g. integration tests using plain `BioParams`) will
  exercise this path and the OutputLogger column will show `-1`.
- ❌ YAML typo in `sea_age_distribution` → caught by strict
  `ALLOWED_OVERRIDE_KEYS` check. Locked by test 8.
- ❌ Out-of-range key (e.g. {0: 1.0}) silently accepted → caught by
  `__post_init__`. Locked by test 2.
- ❌ Distribution doesn't sum to 1.0 → caught by `__post_init__` with
  1e-6 tolerance. Locked by test 2.
- ❌ Numba kernel reads `pool.sea_age` and crashes — N/A because no
  Numba kernel touches `pool.sea_age` in C3.2's scope.
- ❌ Reproduction event writes garbage to `pool.sea_age` for offspring
  → ReproductionEvent doesn't touch `pool.sea_age`; offspring inherit
  the `-1` sentinel from `add_agents`'s default. Documented in this
  spec; no test required.
- ❌ `network.py` `SwitchPopulationEvent` doesn't propagate `sea_age`
  on inter-population transfer → C3.2 explicitly does NOT add this
  propagation (the documented limitation in `network.py:198-208`
  about reach_id encoding mismatch on multi-mesh transfers applies
  identically to sea_age). Pre-C3.2 deployed scenarios use a single
  population per mesh — no impact. Document the limitation in
  `network.py` alongside the existing `origin` propagation note.

**Tests that may need updating:** Possibly `tests/test_output.py` if
it asserts on the exact set of CSV columns. Audit during
implementation. The existing `empty_cols` literal in
`OutputLogger.to_dataframe` is the natural place to add `"sea_age"`,
and any test snapshotting that literal will need updating.

## Out-of-scope (deferred)

- **Sea-age affecting body size at introduction.** Future tier.
  Empirical anchor: ICES WGBAST length-at-age tables, Kallio-Nyberg
  2010 Teno River length-by-sea-age data. Mass scaling via existing
  `LW_a / LW_b` length-weight relationship.
- **Sea-age affecting fecundity.** Future tier; needs separate
  citation chain (Heinimaa & Heinimaa 2003 length-weight-fecundity is
  the existing anchor).
- **Sea-age × sex correlation.** Bouchard 2022 + WGBAST both note
  males more often grilse. Defer to a sex-tracking tier.
- **Year-to-year RRS / sea-age stochasticity.** Single fixed
  distribution per scenario.
- **Sub-stock-specific distributions** (Žeimena vs Simnas). Defer
  until the IBM models sub-stocks within BalticAtlanticSalmon.
- **4SW or older.** Vanishingly rare; not modeled.
- **Origin inheritance on reproduction.** Per C1 scope-OUT.
  Offspring of hatchery parents stay tagged `ORIGIN_WILD` with
  `sea_age = -1`.
- **Inter-population transfer propagation of sea_age** (network.py
  `SwitchPopulationEvent`). The same multi-mesh-encoding caveat that
  applies to `natal_reach_id` and `origin` applies to `sea_age`.
  Document but don't implement. No deployed multi-population scenario
  uses this code path.
- **C3.3 homing precision.** Separate spec.

## YAML schema documentation

The deployed `configs/baltic_salmon_species.yaml` block (current C3.1
state has `pre_spawn_skip_prob` at wild level + `hatchery_overrides:`
with `activity_by_behavior:` and `pre_spawn_skip_prob:`) gets the
C3.2 additions:

```yaml
species:
  BalticAtlanticSalmon:
    # ... ALL existing fields stay (cmax_A, T_OPT, LW_a, LW_b,
    #     activity_by_behavior, pre_spawn_skip_prob, etc.) ...

    # C3.2: sea-age distribution (trinomial 1SW/2SW/3SW)
    # Empirical anchor: WGBAST 2024 wild-stock annual reports —
    # Baltic-wide wild Atlantic salmon return composition. Bothnian
    # Bay / Gulf of Finland populations skew toward 2SW; western
    # Baltic stocks toward 1SW. The 35/55/10 default reflects a
    # weighted mean appropriate to Lithuanian Nemunas-basin
    # Bothnian-style returns.
    # Pre-stocking analog: Kallio-Nyberg, Jokikokko & Jutila 2004
    # (doi:10.1111/j.1439-0426.2004.00491.x) — Simojoki pre-stocking
    # baseline ≈ 30-40% 1SW.
    sea_age_distribution:
      1: 0.35   # 1SW (grilse)
      2: 0.55   # 2SW
      3: 0.10   # 3SW

    hatchery_overrides:
      activity_by_behavior:                  # ← C2
        1: 1.5
        3: 1.875
      pre_spawn_skip_prob: 0.3                # ← C3.1

      # C3.2: sea-age distribution shift in hatchery-stocked salmon.
      # Empirical anchor: Kallio-Nyberg, Jokikokko & Jutila 2004
      # (doi:10.1111/j.1439-0426.2004.00491.x) — Simojoki population
      # showed a measurable reduction in mean sea age of returners
      # following intensive hatchery stocking. Hatchery-stocked
      # Atlantic salmon in the Baltic consistently show a higher
      # 1SW (grilse) fraction and proportional reduction in 2SW/3SW
      # returners.
      # Cross-stock context: Kallio-Nyberg, Vainikka & Heino 2010
      # (doi:10.1111/j.1095-8649.2009.02520.x) — temporal isolation
      # between sea-age ecotypes documents the population-genetic
      # significance of sea-age structure.
      # Population-scale anchor: ICES WGBAST 2024 annual report —
      # Baltic salmon stock assessment with sea-age composition by
      # stock. Hatchery-supported stocks show consistently elevated
      # 1SW fractions vs adjacent wild stocks.
      # Lithuanian Žeimena/Simnas programme (~4-7 generations since
      # 1997) is in a similar generation regime to Kallio-Nyberg
      # 2004's Simojoki post-stocking decades.
      #
      # MECHANISTIC SHAPE: trinomial sampling at IntroductionEvent
      # (the cohort-introduction event) — sea_age is permanent at
      # introduction (matches biology: the marine residency duration
      # is determined before the agent enters the simulated river
      # network).
      #
      # CALIBRATION STATUS: 0.55 1SW share is calibration-grade,
      # bracketed by Kallio-Nyberg 2004's Simojoki post-stocking
      # range. Mandatory sensitivity sweep before publication:
      # hatchery 1SW at {0.35, 0.45, 0.55, 0.65}; cap at 0.7 —
      # beyond that exceeds Simojoki data and conflates sea-age
      # with maturation-rate effects. Wild 1SW share (0.35) is NOT
      # swept (treated as calibration-anchor to WGBAST 2024).
      #
      # SCOPE-OUT (deferred): sea-age → body-size coupling at
      # introduction (future tier needs LW-allometry-by-sea-age
      # citation chain); sea-age → fecundity coupling (future tier);
      # sea-age × sex correlation (no sex tracking in current model);
      # sub-stock-specific distributions (Žeimena vs Simnas);
      # year-to-year stochastic variation; 4SW+ (vanishingly rare).
      sea_age_distribution:
        1: 0.55   # 1SW: +20pp (0.35 → 0.55)
        2: 0.40   # 2SW: -15pp (0.55 → 0.40)
        3: 0.05   # 3SW: -5pp  (0.10 → 0.05)
```
