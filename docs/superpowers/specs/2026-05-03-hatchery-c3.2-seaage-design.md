## Why now

Sea age (1SW = grilse spending 1 winter at sea, 2SW = "two-sea-winter",
3SW = "three-sea-winter") is the most-cited demographic distinction
between Baltic hatchery-stocked vs wild Atlantic salmon (*Salmo
salar*). Hatchery-stocked smolts are released at predictable size and
condition; wild smolts vary more by year-class and natal stream
quality. The empirical signal across the Baltic is consistent:
**hatchery-origin returners have a higher proportion of 1SW (grilse)
than wild-origin returners**, with a corresponding suppression of
older-sea-age cohorts.

This matters for migration timing, body size at return, fecundity,
and reproductive interaction with the C3.1 pre-spawn skip filter.
C3.2 lays the metadata in place; downstream C3.x tiers can read it.

## Scientific basis

**Primary anchor (Baltic-specific, hatchery-vs-wild contrast):**
Jokikokko, Kallio-Nyberg & Jutila (2004), *Journal of Applied
Ichthyology* 20(1):37–42, doi:10.1111/j.1439-0426.2004.00491.x —
*"The timing, sex and age composition of the wild and reared
Atlantic salmon ascending the Simojoki River, northern Finland"*.
The Simojoki population (Bothnian Bay tributary, the closest
unregulated Baltic system to Lithuanian fisheries) is studied for
ascending wild and reared (hatchery) salmon. The paper directly
documents the C3.2 mechanism: **"reared salmon stocked as smolts
produced considerable numbers of ascending one-sea-winter (1 SW)
males, whereas the proportion of male 1 SW salmon was low among
spawning migrants of wild or reared parr origin"**. Reared-as-smolts
fish (the modern Baltic stocking practice) have a markedly higher
1SW fraction than wild-origin fish. This is the strongest
single empirical anchor for C3.2's intervention shape.

**Secondary anchor (life-history divergence in Baltic stocks):**
Kallio-Nyberg, Vainikka & Heino (2010), *Journal of Fish Biology*
76(3):622–640, doi:10.1111/j.1095-8649.2009.02520.x —
*"Divergent trends in life-history traits between Atlantic salmon
Salmo salar of wild and hatchery origin in the Baltic Sea"*. Studies
four Baltic *S. salar* stocks 1972-1995 with varying breeding
history. **"Maturation probabilities controlled for water temperature,
L_T at capture and L_T at release had increased in all stocks. The
least change was observed in the River Tornionjoki S. salar that was
subject only to supportive stockings originating from wild parents.
These results suggest a long-term divergence between semi-natural and
broodstock-based S. salar stocks."** Establishes the population-genetic
significance of broodstock-based hatchery practices on sea-age and
maturation in Baltic Atlantic salmon.

**Supplementary anchor (environmental modulation):** Kallio-Nyberg,
Saloniemi & Koljonen (2020), *Journal of Applied Ichthyology*
36(3):288–297, doi:10.1111/jai.14033 — *"Increasing temperature
associated with increasing grilse proportion and smaller grilse size
of Atlantic salmon"*. Carlin-tag analysis (1985-2014) of northern
Baltic salmon shows that **"warmer spring temperatures during the
smolt year were associated with a higher proportion of one-sea-winter
(1SW) males during the return migration"**. C3.2's static distribution
parameters are an unconditional baseline; year-to-year temperature
modulation is a documented but explicitly out-of-scope refinement.

**Population-scale anchor:** ICES Working Group on Baltic Salmon and
Trout (WGBAST) annual reports tabulate sea-age composition by stock
and year and consistently report higher 1SW fractions in
hatchery-supported stocks than in adjacent wild stocks. WGBAST is
the population-scale reference for the wild and hatchery defaults
below; sub-stock granularity is not yet modeled in the IBM.

**Generation-level applicability to the Lithuanian programme:** The
Žeimena/Simnas programme has been running since 1997 (~4-7 salmon
generations). This places Lithuanian hatchery fish in a similar
generation-level regime to the Simojoki post-stocking decades
analysed by Jokikokko et al. (2004) and the Baltic broodstock-based
stocks analysed by Kallio-Nyberg et al. (2010), making both Bothnian
Bay studies defensible analogs for Nemunas-basin defaults pending
direct Lithuanian sea-age-by-origin measurement.

## Sentinel discipline

`pool.sea_age == SEA_AGE_UNSET (-1)` indicates "marine residency not
applicable" — offspring (set by ReproductionEvent's add_agents
default), pre-introduction agents, OR non-Baltic legacy scenarios.
Any consumer reading `pool.sea_age` MUST first mask `pool.sea_age >=
SEA_AGE_1SW (1)` or use `Population.adult_sea_age_mask()`. Future
C3.x specs that wire sea_age into mass/fecundity MUST add this
assertion at the top of their kernel and a regression test asserting
that an offspring agent leaking into the consumer's index set
raises rather than silently consuming the sentinel as a valid age.

The sentinel is `int8 -1`, which would silently:
- Multiply by `-1` if used as a scaling factor (negative output).
- Index `lookup[-1]` (Python wraps to last element; offspring
  silently get the *3SW* lookup value).

These hazards motivate the `adult_sea_age_mask()` helper and the
sentinel-discipline policy.

## Divergent values

```python
# Wild baseline:
sea_age_distribution = {1: 0.35, 2: 0.55, 3: 0.10}

# Hatchery (full-replacement):
sea_age_distribution = {1: 0.55, 2: 0.40, 3: 0.05}
```

**Wild baseline provenance:** 35% 1SW / 55% 2SW / 10% 3SW reflects
WGBAST annual-report ranges for wild-origin Baltic Atlantic salmon
stocks, weighted toward Bothnian Bay populations as the closest
analog for Lithuanian Nemunas-basin returns. Aligned with the
"wild or reared parr origin" comparison group in Jokikokko 2004
(low 1SW male fraction).

**Hatchery shift:** 55% 1SW / 40% 2SW / 5% 3SW represents the
Jokikokko 2004 Simojoki "reared-stocked-as-smolts" finding:
considerably higher 1SW fraction with proportional reduction in
older sea-ages. Hatchery 3SW is suppressed more strongly than 2SW
because earlier marine maturation eliminates the tail of late
returners disproportionately.

**Effect size at default:** for an introduced cohort of N hatchery
agents, expected sea_age composition shifts from (35/55/10) →
(55/40/5) relative to the wild scenario. In a mixed scenario with
k% hatchery, the population-level grilse fraction increases by
~`0.20 × k%`. This compounds with C2's activity divergence and
C3.1's pre-spawn skip to produce a distinguishable hatchery vs
wild signal in cohort tracking.

**Calibration sensitivity:** the hatchery 1SW share of 0.55 is
calibration-grade. Mandatory sensitivity sweep before publication:
**hatchery 1SW at {0.35, 0.45, 0.55, 0.65}** (with 2SW/3SW
renormalised proportionally to keep total = 1.0). **Cap at 0.7** —
beyond is unsupported by Jokikokko 2004's Simojoki data and
would conflate sea-age effects with maturation-rate effects (a
separate biological signal).

The wild 1SW share of 0.35 is NOT swept (treated as
calibration-anchor to WGBAST wild baseline; varying it would change
what the contrast is measured *against*).

## Choice of approach

Three options were considered for the model intervention shape
(brainstormed 2026-05-03):

1. **Metadata-only sea-age tag at introduction, trinomial
   1SW/2SW/3SW distribution** (selected) — `IntroductionEvent.execute`
   and `PatchIntroductionEvent.execute` sample `sea_age` from
   origin-aware distribution; no consumer event yet. Mirrors C1's
   "tag now, consume later" architecture exactly.
2. **Sea-age field PLUS body-size coupling at introduction** —
   sample sea_age, then set `initial_mass_mean` conditional on
   sea_age. **REJECTED:** doubles scope; introduces a new biological
   signal (sea_age → size) that is itself calibration-sensitive.
3. **Continuous sea-age (months at sea) with categorical 1SW/2SW/3SW
   binning** — Most flexible. **REJECTED:** empirical data is
   reported as discrete classes; the IBM has no time-of-introduction
   model that needs continuous sea-residence.

**Approach 1 selected** for: (a) cleanest mapping to Jokikokko 2004's
empirical shape (categorical 1SW vs MSW by origin), (b) symmetry
with C1's tag-only metadata pattern, (c) zero touch on bioenergetics
or migration hot loops, (d) reuses C2's HatcheryDispatch + landscape
injection pattern (extended once for `species_config`).

## Risk + regression surface

**Behaviour change for pre-C3.2 scenarios: zero (after the
landscape entry is wired).**

- YAMLs without `sea_age_distribution` → dataclass default
  `{1: 0.35, 2: 0.55, 3: 0.10}` → existing tests still load.
- `IntroductionEvent`/`PatchIntroductionEvent` without a Baltic
  `species_config` → `is_baltic=False` → WILD agents get
  `sea_age=SEA_AGE_UNSET`; HATCHERY agents raise (only
  reachable in misconfigured scenarios).
- `pool.sea_age` initialised to `SEA_AGE_UNSET` for offspring +
  non-Baltic scenarios. New OutputLogger CSV column; downstream
  consumers parsing positionally need a one-time update.
  `tests/test_output.py:28` uses `set(df.columns) >= {...}`
  superset check — no break.
- `SwitchPopulationEvent` propagation: transferred adults retain
  their sea_age (mirrors origin propagation).

**Behaviour change for C3.2-aware scenarios: by design.**

- WILD agents drawn from `{1: 0.35, 2: 0.55, 3: 0.10}` at introduction.
- HATCHERY agents drawn from `{1: 0.55, 2: 0.40, 3: 0.05}`.
- Compound effect with C2 + C3.1 produces distinguishable signal.

**Silent-failure modes (closed by design):**

- ❌ Landscape missing `species_config` key → CLOSED by simulation.py
  TypedDict + step() landscape construction edits. Locked by
  test 5/6 (which exercise full landscape construction).
- ❌ `hasattr` proxy admitting non-Baltic objects with same-named
  attribute → CLOSED by `isinstance(species_cfg.wild,
  BalticBioParams)`. Locked by test 7.
- ❌ HATCHERY origin in non-Baltic config silently emits `-1`s →
  CLOSED by explicit `elif self.origin == ORIGIN_HATCHERY: raise`.
  Locked by test 8.
- ❌ Partial-merge silent-no-op (override that happens to merge to
  wild) → CLOSED by full-replacement semantics. Locked by test 4.
- ❌ ARRAY_FIELDS adds field but `add_agents` fill block leaves
  garbage → CLOSED by explicit `new_arrays["sea_age"][old_n:] =
  sea_age` line. Locked by test 5/6 (sample exact counts) and test
  10 (compact preservation).
- ❌ PatchIntroductionEvent `rng` undefined → CLOSED by explicit
  `rng = landscape.get("rng", ...)` add. Locked by test 9.
- ❌ `SwitchPopulationEvent` silently demotes transferred adults to
  `-1` → CLOSED by sea_age propagation. Locked by test 11.
- ❌ Out-of-range or non-summing distribution silently accepted →
  CLOSED by `__post_init__`. Locked by test 2.
- ❌ Bool key (`type(True) is int` is False but `isinstance(True,
  int)` is True) silently accepted → CLOSED by `type(k) is int`.
  Locked by test 2.
- ❌ Future C3.x consumers treat `-1` as a valid sea_age →
  CLOSED-BY-CONVENTION via "Sentinel discipline" section + the
  `Population.adult_sea_age_mask()` helper; future tier specs
  must add the assertion.

**Tests that may need updating:** none expected. The existing
output test uses superset check; reproduction event leaves sea_age
at default; pre-C3.2 reproduction tests bypass sampling entirely.
